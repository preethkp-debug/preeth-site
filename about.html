About

I'm Preeth.
Operator first.
Consultant second.

The short version

Eight years building my own media business taught me how growth actually works.

I bootstrapped TechPlugged from a one-person blog into a publication that reaches a few million tech readers every month. No funding, no team of writers for the first three years, no agency — just compounding decisions made over a long time.

Along the way I learned SEO at the level you only learn it when your livelihood depends on the next algorithm update. I learned paid media because organic alone has a ceiling. I learned brand because at some point being correct stops being enough.

Today I bring that same lens to a small, deliberate roster of brands. I don't run an agency. There's no account manager. You'll work with me directly.

A condensed history

The road here, briefly.

2016
First freelance SEO clients
A handful of local businesses, an obsession with crawl budgets, and the realisation that I liked this more than my engineering job.
2018
TechPlugged is founded
Started writing one tech post a day on a self-hosted WordPress blog. The first two years averaged 600 readers a month. I kept publishing.
2020
First million-view month
Crossed a million pageviews for the first time on the back of a single review. Quit my job the same week.
2022
Consulting roster opens
Started taking on a small number of advisory clients — mostly SaaS founders who'd been reading the blog and wanted help with their own content engines.
2024
AI workflows go production
Built the first end-to-end AI editorial pipeline for a Series B SaaS client — moved them from 8 posts a month to 70 with the same headcount.
2026
Where we are now
Working with founder-led brands across the US, UK and India on growth, AI ops and editorial systems. TechPlugged still ships every day.
How I work

Six things I won't compromise on.

01

Direct work

You'll talk to me, not an account manager. The person doing the thinking is the person doing the work.

02

Honest reads

If your category is unwinnable on the budget you have, I'll tell you on the discovery call. I'd rather lose the engagement than lose your year.

03

Systems over heroics

One-off campaigns are easy. The hard work is building a system your team can run after I'm gone — that's what I sell.

04

No category absolutes

I read every paid media bro telling you "creative is the only lever." It isn't. Distribution, offer, audience and pricing are also levers. We pull the right one.

05

AI as a tool, not a thesis

I build with LLMs every day, but I don't think they're magic. They're a 10× multiplier on a workflow you've already designed well — and a 10× amplifier on one you haven't.

06

Knowledge transfer

Every engagement ends with a written, version-controlled playbook. Your team should be able to keep running after the retainer wraps. That's the actual deliverable.
